Key Cybersecurity Concepts

Phishing

Deceptive emails, texts, or cloned websites designed to steal your information. They often create a sense of urgency.

Social Engineering

The art of manipulating people into giving up confidential information. Tactics include pretexting, baiting, and tailgating.

White Hat Hacking

Ethical hackers who find security vulnerabilities before malicious attackers do. A growing and important career path.

Password Safety

Use long, unique passphrases for each account and store them securely in a password manager. Avoid common words.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

A second layer of security. Use authenticator apps or physical security keys over SMS for the best protection.

Spot the Red Flags

Phishing emails try to trick you. Hover over the numbered red flags on this example to see what to look out for.

1
Urgent Subject Line: Attackers create a sense of panic to make you act without thinking. "Account Suspension" is a common tactic.
2
Generic Greeting: Vague greetings like "Dear Valued Customer" are a red flag. Legitimate companies usually address you by name.
3
Suspicious Link: Hover over links before clicking. Does the URL look strange or misspelled? Does it match the supposed sender?
4
Unexpected Attachments: Never open attachments you weren't expecting, even if they seem to be from someone you know. They can contain malware.

About the NJCCIC

The New Jersey Cybersecurity and Communications Integration Cell (NJCCIC) is the state's one-stop-shop for cybersecurity. It provides threat intelligence, best practices, and incident response for residents, businesses, and government.

Youth programs include the Cyber Safety Patrol, Cadetships, High School Internships, and Capture the Flag (CTF) events to build the next generation of cyber defenders.

Tools & Resources

Spam Blockers

Tools like Nomorobo, Truecaller, and Hiya can help filter out spam calls and texts.

Password Managers

Securely store your passwords with trusted managers like Bitwarden or 1Password.

Antivirus Software

Protect your devices with reputable antivirus like Malwarebytes, Bitdefender, or the built-in Windows Defender.
